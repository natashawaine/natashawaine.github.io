About, DPub, Contact, Natasha Waine
1964
Humanity has been developing technologies that are progressively becoming more invasive toward the human body. We, as a human race have gradually been turning into data that the computer can study. As it stands, it is a very plausible reality that biometrics and tracking will be ingrained into everyday life, and that one’s ‘identity’ will guide relationships with current services. The body, so to speak, becomes the ‘key’ to unlock and conduct all interactions with the environmetn around us. In creating ‘security’, one loses their anonymity and privacy defined by mere statistics and numbers.
Notes
1964, the year of facial recognition’s invention (or so is speculated – an unnamed intelligence withheld this publication as it were). Regardless, this was the first time the computer could begin recognising the human face. The technology required marking the face manually. Thanks to Woody Bledsoe, Helen Chan Wolf and Charles Bisson, software has since catapulted into highly intelligent recognition systems.
